# A 15-Year Analysis of Food Price Inflation (2008 - 2026)

This project provides a comprehensive analysis of food price volatility and economic trends in Myanmar over a 15-year period. Utilizing official World Food Programme (WFP) data, I tracked the cost of essential commodities to identify patterns of inflation and regional price disparities during periods of significant socioeconomic change.

# üìä Key Insights
 * Extreme Inflation: Identified a significant structural break in price stability starting in 2022, with staple goods like Rice and Palm Oil seeing triple-digit percentage increases in local currency (MMK).
 * Regional Anomalies: Through SQL analysis, I discovered that Chin State and Rakhine State consistently record the highest prices for vegetables and oils, likely due to supply chain disruptions.
 * Record Highs: The most expensive food item recorded was Onions (local) in March 2025, reaching 45,000 MMK per KG in remote markets.

# üöÄ Business Recommendation
Based on the trend analysis, food security interventions should prioritize border regions where transport costs drive local prices 3-4x higher than the national average. Stabilizing the cost of low-quality rice should be the primary focus for regional economic policy.
